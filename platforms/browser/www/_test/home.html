Chinese Language:
  • Mandarin Chinese is the most popular language in China, with over 955 million speakers out of China’s total population of 1.21 billion people.
  • Another language that has official status in China is Cantonese is used as the official language in Hong Kong and Macau
  • Cantonese has about 80 million speakers across the country
  • In Hong Kong, English is established as an official language and is used in both print and electronic media
  • Another major international language in China is Portuguese, which is used as the official language in Macau
Chinese characters:
  • Traditional Chinese characters are used in Hong Kong, Macau, and the Republic of China (Taiwan).
  • The traditional characters can still be read and understood by many mainland Chinese
  • Overseas Chinese communities generally tend to use traditional characters
HSK:
  • Chinese Proficiency Test (HSK), an international standardized test of Chinese language proficiency,assesses non-native Chinese speakers’ abilities in using the Chinese language in their daily, academic and professional lives.
  • HSK 1: 150 words
  • HSK 2: 150 words
  • HSK 3: 300 words
  • HSK 4: 600 words
  • HSK 5: 1300 words
  • HSK 6: 2500 words
  • HSK 1 - 4: 1200 words
  • HSK 1 - 6: 5000 words
Pinyin:
  • Pinyin is the official romanization system for Standard Chinese in mainland China and to some extent in Taiwan
